About Us

Who We Are

The Bellingham Unity Committee was founded in 2018 by Terrance (Teejay) Morris to celebrate and remember important people and events in the history of the African diaspora. Since our founding, we have organized annual Juneteenth celebrations, Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Unity Balls, the Ascendants of the Movement Black History Month events, and the 2020 Peaceful Solidarity Rally, which drew over 5,000 attendees.

Our founding committee members include: Beth Andrews, Geneva Blake, Dede and KeyKey Davis, Courtney Ellis, Amanda Grelock, EmJaye Johnston, Monea Kerr, Ryan Peters, Alex McIntyre, Eve Smason Marcus, and Jesi van Leeuwen.

Our Mission

To improve the quality of our community by enhancing, teaching, and honoring the rich legacy and history of African American culture — building a more equitable and inclusive Whatcom County through celebration, community, and education.

Organizational Support

The Bellingham Unity Committee receives fiscal sponsorship from the Whatcom Human Rights Task Force, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. We also receive project assistance from the Community Food Co-op, Bruna Press + Archive, and Localgroup Studio.
